Terrain Map: New Zealand Bike Race

This was created in a design course taken at University of Wisconsin-Madison as part of my master's degree. The point of the project was to create a bike race over 21 days with varying terrain somewhere in the world. I choose New Zealand. I used hillshades and land cover data in ArcMap and Photoshop to manipulate the overlay. Then used Illustrator to customize the vector data.

Developmental Toxicity of Triclosan on Zebrafish

I conducted research as an undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin - La Crosse. My research was evaluating the developmental toxicity of Triclosan (TCS) on zebrafish. The chemical was previously shown to be harmful to zebrafish larvae but was also shown in a different study to absorb to dissolved organic carbon (DOC). My hypothesis was that co-exposure to TCS in DOC would reduce TCS's bioavailability and therefore the toxicity to zebrafish larvae.

My study showed that there was not a significant change in toxic effects however; there was a dose-response when TCS and DOC were present. This research led to a publication in the journal Zebrafish. As well as several conference presentations at the National Conference for Undergraduate Research and the Society of Environmental Toxicology and Chemistry.

Evaluation of Urban Sprawl in Carver County, MN

This poster was created as part of a project in Remote Sensing taken at University of Wisconsin - La Crosse. My project compared the land use of Carver County, MN from 2003 and 2011. I used satellite imagery (LANDSAT), aerial photography and an unsupervised classification (ISODATA) of the satellite data to determine land use. Urban land cover had the largest increase indicating an urban sprawl in this county.
